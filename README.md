# 💫 About Me:
Hey 👋 I am Anugrah, a B.Tech student at Sahrdaya College of Engineering and Technology (SCET) with an unshakeable belief that great technology comes from great collaboration. I don’t just build digital solutions, I create experiences that connect people and solve real problems. I believe in the power of connection, and through working with teams and interacting with people in tech communities, I’ve learned that the best innovations happen when different perspectives come together.<br><br>Currently I’m a Computer Science student who spends a lot of time experimenting with code, building projects, and participating in hackathons. I enjoy starting with a rough concept, figuring things out step by step, and slowly shaping it into something functional and meaningful. Many of my projects begin from curiosity. Sometimes it becomes a simulation, sometimes a web app, sometimes a small game or an experimental tool, but every project is an opportunity to explore new technologies and creative ideas.<br><br>Hackathons are probably where I enjoy building the most. There’s something exciting about working with limited time, brainstorming solutions with a team, and turning an idea into a working prototype within hours. The pressure, creativity, and collaboration make the whole experience incredibly rewarding.<br><br>Most of the work you’ll find here reflects that mindset of experimenting, learning, building, improving, and occasionally breaking things along the way. Some projects are polished, some are prototypes, and some are simply ideas that were too interesting not to try. Each one represents a step in the process of learning and exploring what technology can do.<br><br>Right now I’m focused on strengthening my fundamentals in computer science while continuing to build projects that challenge me technically and creatively. I’m particularly interested in web technologies, simulations, problem solving, and systems that combine logic with interactive design.<br><br>Outside of academics, I enjoy collaborating on interesting ideas, participating in tech events, and exploring projects that push me to learn something new. For me, coding is not just about writing programs, it’s about creating something that didn’t exist before. If you enjoy building cool things, experimenting with technology, or working on interesting projects, feel free to connect or collaborate.<br>
